INTRODUCTION

I created a Garage Sale App for Google as a design exercise. I chose this prompt because I was intrigued by the challenge of designing an app that had multiple use casethes (seller & buyer).

ROLE

I created this app over the course of 5 days. I conducted user reasearch with interviews and competitive analysis. I designed the user flow, digital wireframes and high fidelity screens.

01. RESEARCH

INTERVIEWS & COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

I had never held my own garage sale in the past, and thought it would be an interesting challenge to tackle. I wanted to gain a solid understanding of what the problems of holding a garage sale was. I decided to target college students that were graduating who needed to get rid of their things.


I also did competitive analysis on various websites that dealt with providing a platform for people to buy and sell items such as Craigstlist and Etsy. I thought it would also be valuable to do competitive analysis on websites that helped users sell products at a competitive price such as StubHub and eBay.

02. SKETCHES

WIREFRAMING

I always begin making wireframes in my sketchbook. For this project, I made all my product decisions here before going into the digital wireframes. There were five main problems I found from the interviews.


(1) It is time consuming to post on various social media outlets. (2) There is big time pressure, and many students ended up giving away their things for free or threw them away. (3) Most people do not have the necessary transportation, which was one of the biggest factors people could not purchase items. (4) Even with transportation, often times, students would sell on Craigstlist and many people were far too away that it was not worth driving the product over. (5) It ishard to coordinate with people, especially when using various outlets to sell multiple items.

DIGITAL WIREFRAMES

SIGN UP FLOW

PROFILE

The main screen shows users the item, price and appoximately how far away the location is. This wil help them decide if the purchase will be possible or not depending on their proximity.

CHAT FLOW

On the main chat feed, users can see the people they are buying or selling items to in chronological order. It shows you an image of what product you are talking about so you can quickly find the person you want to talk to. You can also reply to them directly on this screen if it is a quick reply.

SEARCH FLOW

TRANSPORTATION FLOW

It also provides users access to transportation directly so they can reserve it as they purchase the item.

ADD LISTING FLOW

If users are unaware how to price their items, the app gives them suggestions and how much similar items have sold for in the past.

DONATE FLOW

Out of the five people I interviewed, all of them said they ended up giving things away for free or throwing them away. The app will have a setting by which users can donate their items as well.

04. IN RETROSPECT

A REFLECTION


I think it would be valuable for this app to have been a web app because it would have provided users the ability to use this app without having to download it. In addition, with a web app, I could have used their search history to see what kind of furniture they were looking for. I think there could have been an easier way for users to be able to add a listing, rather than going to the search page to add a product.
