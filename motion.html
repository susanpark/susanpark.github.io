Time, Motion & Communication

Dan Boyarski has been teaching a class called Time, Motion & Communication at Carnegie Mellon University for the past 30 years. We learn about the art of kinetic typography, as well as the fundamentals of motion graphics using Adobe After Effects.

After Effects

I have been interested in motion graphics and film since I was younger, but never got a chance to explore my capabilities until this class. These projects show the range of styles I can create.

Rhythm

We were to create a video that visualized the sound of the music we chose. I wanted to pick a piece that had an explosive and memorable beat. I chose Scriptina by Losco because it emphasizes the contrast of small noises and loud drops. I also wanted to incorporate animation of characters within the video. Krieger is one of my favorite characters from a show called Archer. He portrays an odd character who loves taking people apart to turn them into cyborgs. I wanted to progress from simple shapes to transform and use his face to explode and distort.

STORYBOARDING

I storyboarded many ideas in my moleskin before starting the video. I had to create the different pieces to explode individually. I drew most of the shapes out and converted them in illustrator. I colored them according to how they would look in a 3D space. I chose to keep all of my illustrations simple because I wanted to emphasize the explosions, and not draw attention to detail of the shapes. I also illustrated Krieger and Archer's face.

An Introduction

Zachary Bergeron is a student at Carnegie Mellon University. I wanted to introduce him in a light that not many people know him from. He is a very different person under his quiet and more serious facade. I wanted to use this video to also encourage him to pursue what he has wanted to do for a while, but does not have the courage to.

Kinetic Typography

Kinetic and moving typography is an important aspect of motion graphics. I chose Leonardo DiCaprio's monologue from The Wolf of Wall Street and used only typography to visualize his speech.
